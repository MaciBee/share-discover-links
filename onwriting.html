Ricky Gervais: write about what you known

Probably the greatest single influence on my creative process is my old english teacher said to me when I was about 13-14. He just said write about what you know

I wrote in every detail- the smell of tea, lavendar and mold..i did it all...

It sort of taught me that being honest is what counts. trying to make the ordinary, extraordinary is so much better than starting w/ the extraordinary. Because it doesn't really connect.

If something is real for one person, it's touched their life, I think as a creator and director, it's your job to make an audience as excited and facinated about a subject as you are. And real life does that.
